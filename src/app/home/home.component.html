After nine successful years of hosting Durga Puja and connecting New England communities, Boston Durga Bari welcomes you to yet another fun-packed four-day long event. As usual, our goal is to foster an open and collaborative platform for patrons to connect at various levels. This sense of camaraderie helps us to keep the event non-ticketed. We only rely on voluntary donations which help to keep the wheels spinning.

Please review the calendar of events and timing for each day below. Join us in the uber-casual group chats, fondly called adda, while you savour the volunteer cooked meals. This year due to Covid related restrictions, we need prior registration for visitors. Please check out FAQ section for details.

We look forward to seeing you at the pandal.

 Salutation

सर्वमङ्गलमाङ्गल्ये शिवे सर्वार्थसाधिके ।
शरण्ये त्र्यम्बके गौरि नारायणि नमोऽस्तु ते ॥

She is the most auspicious one and the one who bestows auspiciousness upon the entire world. She is pure and holy and protects those who surrender to her. With her three eyes, she is the Mother of the three worlds and is known as Gauri, daughter of the mountain king. We bow down to thee….Mother Durga.
